红牛能给德国队添上翅膀吗？6月29日，德国队被逐出世界杯后，德国足协转向尤尔根·克洛普，请他执掌国家队。这位名帅——已原则上同意接手——目前是这家奥地利公司的全球足球主管，负责监管其遍布世界的八家俱乐部。

数十年来，这家全球领先的能量饮料制造商在从足球到一级方程式赛车的各类运动中，扮演了远超其本业的角色。这是红牛创始人迪特里希·马特希茨大力推行的营销战略。上世纪80年代，马特希茨以泰国能量滋补饮料Krating Daeng为蓝本，改造出自己的饮品。（其创造者的继承人尤维迪亚家族持有红牛51%的股份。）这位奥地利人被证明拥有罕见的营销天赋。Red Bull verleiht Flüüügel（红牛给你翅膀）是他的主意。赞助奥地利跳伞运动员费利克斯·鲍姆加特纳也是他的主意；2012年，后者从平流层中的氦气球跃下，坠向地球。

马特希茨于2022年去世时，有人认为红牛会像它崛起时那样迅速坠落。他没有明确的继任者：一个三人董事会接管了管理事务，三人都行事低调。（马特希茨之子马克继承了他49%的股份，但不参与经营。）

然而红牛继续高飞。去年它售出140亿罐，较2024年增长10%。它占据全球市场28%的份额，远领先于主要竞争对手怪兽的17%。它之所以还能悬在高空，主要靠的是坚持马特希茨的战略。即便如此，来自监管者和新竞争者的威胁已然逼近。

如今执掌红牛的三人小组仍坚守市场较高价位。一罐标准250毫升装在美国最大超市沃尔玛售价约2.40美元。公司仍将制造外包给长期商业伙伴：另一家奥地利公司Rauch Fruchtsäfte负责调配和罐装饮料；美国波尔公司供应铝罐。这帮助红牛保持无债状态。

红牛继续将约30%的销售额用于营销——去年超过30亿欧元（34亿美元）。全球软饮料龙头可口可乐的这一比例约为10%，怪兽为7.5%。它避开传统广告，而是通过红牛媒体公司制作自己的内容；该公司是奥地利仅次于国有广播机构ORF的最大媒体公司，旗下包括一个电视网、电影和电视工作室、一家唱片公司以及数本杂志。

并非人人都是粉丝。有人批评它赞助极限运动。近二十名运动员在表演其特技时丧生。去年，欧盟委员会开始调查红牛是否违反了欧盟竞争规则，利用其支配地位向零售商施压，要求其停止销售竞争对手的商品，或把这些商品摆在不利位置。

<h1>How high can Red Bull fly?</h1>
<h3>The world’s most formidable marketing machine faces growing threats</h3>
<h3>July 16th 2026</h3>
<p>CAn Red Bull give die Mannschaft wiiings? After Germany was bundled out of the World Cup on June 29th the national football federation turned to Jürgen Klopp to take charge of the team. The celebrated coach—who has agreed in principle to take the job—is currently the Austrian company’s “global head of soccer”, overseeing eight clubs around the world.</p>
<p>Over the decades the world’s leading maker of energy drinks has come to play an outsize role in all manner of sports, from football to Formula One. It is a marketing strategy that Dietrich Mateschitz, Red Bull’s founder, pursued with gusto. Mateschitz adapted his drink in the 1980s from Krating Daeng, a Thai energy tonic. (The Yoovidhya family, its creator’s heirs, own 51% of Red Bull.) The Austrian proved to have a rare talent for marketing. “Red Bull verleiht Flüüügel” (”Red Bull gives you wiiings”) was his idea. So was sponsoring Felix Baumgartner, an Austrian skydiver who in 2012 jumped to Earth from a helium balloon in the stratosphere.</p>
<p>When Mateschitz died in 2022, some thought Red Bull would fall as quickly as it had risen. He had no clear successor: a three-man board, all of whom keep a low profile, took over management. (Mateschitz’s son Mark inherited his 49% stake, but has no operative role.)</p>
<p>Yet Red Bull has continued to fly. Last year it sold 14bn cans, up by 10% from 2024. It has 28% of the global market, well ahead of Monster, its main rival, with 17%. It has stayed aloft mainly by sticking to Mateschitz’s strategy. Even so, threats from regulators and new competitors loom.</p>
<p>The triumvirate that now runs Red Bull has kept to the pricier end of the market. A standard 250ml can costs around $2.40 at Walmart, America’s biggest supermarket. The company still outsources manufacturing to long-term business partners: Rauch Fruchtsäfte, another Austrian firm, mixes and cans the drinks; America’s Ball Corporation supplies the aluminium cans. This has helped Red Bull stay debt-free.</p>
<p>Red Bull continues to spend around 30% of sales on marketing—more than €3bn ($3.4bn) last year. Coca-Cola, the world leader in soft drinks, spends around 10% and Monster 7.5%. It eschews conventional advertising, instead making its own content through Red Bull Media House, Austria’s biggest media company after ORF, the state broadcaster. It encompasses a TV network, film and TV studios, a record label and several magazines.</p>
<p>Not everybody is a fan. Some criticise its sponsorship of extreme sports. Almost two dozen athletes have died while performing its stunts. Last year the European Commission began investigating whether Red Bull had broken EU competition rules by using its dominant position to press retailers to stop selling rivals’ wares or to place them unfavourably.</p>
<p>Another line of criticism concerns the side-effects of its signature product, which contains caffeine, taurine and sugar. The company says that “health authorities around the world have concluded that Red Bull is safe to consume,” and points out that a 250ml can contains the same amount of caffeine as a cup of home-brewed coffee. But studies show that, even in moderate amounts, it is harmful for children, as it increases heart rate and blood pressure. The American Academy of Paediatrics and the Food and Drug Administration recommend that children should avoid energy drinks. Latvia, Lithuania and Poland are among the countries that ban their sale to under-18s. Britain is mulling doing so for under-16s. For its part, Red Bull notes that energy drinks “are not recommended for children”, and that it does not market its products to them.</p>
<p>New competitors are eager to position themselves as a healthier alternative. Celsius, an American brand, contains no added sugar and far fewer calories than Red Bull (but more than twice the caffeine). Known to some as “Red Bull for women”, it has become the number three energy drink in America, after Red Bull and Monster.</p>
<p>For now, Red Bull remains on top. When Mateschitz originally adapted Krating Daeng to European palates, experts told him that no one would want it. He ignored them, charged a premium price for the concoction and built a market for it based largely on its adventurous image. You might call it gravity-defying. <span class="span_ufinish">■</span></p>
