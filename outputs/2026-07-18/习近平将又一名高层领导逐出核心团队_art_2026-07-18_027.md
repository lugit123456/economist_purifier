### 🔍 核心观点与论据拆解
- 中国政治体制一向强调稳定，核心领导层通常每五年调整一次；但《经济学人》指出，习近平正以毛泽东时代以来未见的频率对自己团队成员下手，最新落马者是曾任新疆党委书记的马兴瑞。
- 官方媒体称，马兴瑞因极其严重腐败被开除党籍，案件移交检方；他曾是受赞誉的航天工程师，参与监督探月项目，但长期缺席重大活动，2026年4月已被宣布涉嫌严重违纪违法受查。
- 党方公开指控马兴瑞收受巨额贿赂、搞大规模家庭腐败，并利用权力和金钱换取性好处。文章指出，后毛泽东时代被逐出政治局者几乎总被控贪腐，因为这比披露可能存在的政治紧张更易向公众解释。
- 文章同时强调，本案中几乎没有官方暗示或可信传闻显示马兴瑞与习近平存在严重政治摩擦，因此严重腐败本身或足以解释其被清除；但马兴瑞已无法公开回应或否认相关指控。
- 自2022年最近一次五年一度党代会以来，两名国防部长、一名外交部长和数十名高级军官被清洗；军队两名最高级将领中，何卫东已被逐出政治局，张又侠正受调查且几乎肯定会失去政治局席位。

<h1>Xi Jinping expels another leader from his top team</h1>
<h3>He has set a record for booting out members of the Politburo</h3>
<h3>July 16th 2026</h3>
<p>CHINA’S POLITICAL system prides itself on stability. Reshuffles of core leaders normally happen only every five years. But Xi Jinping has been <a href="https://www.economist.com/leaders/2026/01/29/xi-jinpings-purge-should-worry-the-world">taking the axe</a> to members of his team at a rate not seen since the days of Mao Zedong. The latest to fall is Ma Xingrui, who until last year was the Communist Party boss of the far-western region of Xinjiang. State media said he had been stripped of his party membership for “extremely serious” corruption and his case handed to prosecutors. Another seat on the ruling Politburo—now down to 21 active members from its initial 24—has thus fallen vacant.</p>
<p>Mr Ma’s downfall is no surprise. Once a celebrated aerospace engineer who helped oversee lunar-exploration projects, he has long been absent from major events. In April the party said he was being investigated for “serious violations” of its rules and the law. Now it has publicly accused him of accepting “huge amounts” in bribes, engaging in “large-scale family corruption” and using his power and money to gain sexual favours.</p>
<p>Since the Mao era, charges of graft have almost always been laid against those expelled from the Politburo. Such wrongdoing is easier to explain to the public than revealing what is often suspected to be the main reason: political tension with the party chief. In this case, however, there has been little official hint or plausible rumour of serious political friction between Mr Ma and Mr Xi. Egregious corruption may be enough to explain his ousting—not that Mr Ma will be able to comment publicly or deny the charges against him.</p>
<p>His removal from the Politburo is a reminder of Mr Xi’s willingness to lash out. Since the most recent five-yearly party congress in 2022, two defence ministers, a foreign minister and dozens of high-ranking military officers have been purged. The armed forces’ two most senior generals have fallen: He Weidong was expelled from the Politburo and Zhang Youxia is under investigation and all but certain to lose his Politburo seat, too. Since Mr Xi took power in 2012, three Politburo members have been dismissed—the most under any leader post-Mao. (The other was Sun Zhengcai in 2017, once considered to be a potential successor to Mr Xi.)</p>
<p>Mr Ma can expect, at a minimum, to spend a very long time behind bars. No former Politburo member has been executed, but that is an unreliable guide to his likely fate. Mr Xi is a norm-breaker. <span class="span_ufinish">■</span></p>
