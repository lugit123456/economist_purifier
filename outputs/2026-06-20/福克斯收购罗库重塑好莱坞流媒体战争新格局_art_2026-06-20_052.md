商业｜福克斯出击

福克斯、罗库与流媒体战争的下一阶段

一笔220亿美元的交易在好莱坞造就一股可畏新势力

2026年6月18日

流媒体战争——好莱坞持续数年的内容支出混战——的主要赢家之一，结果竟是个未参战者。在迪士尼等公司为打造流媒体服务、与奈飞之流竞争而大撒金钱之际，福克斯一直按兵不动。这家有线电视巨头的大部分收入仍来自老派的线性电视；过去五年，其股价上涨近40%，而同期迪士尼的股价跌幅也大致相当。

6月15日，福克斯出人意料地宣布，已同意收购美国公司罗库，从而大举迈入流媒体业务。罗库销售流媒体硬件和软件，并运营自己的流媒体频道。这笔以现金和股票计价、估值约220亿美元的交易，标志着福克斯战略转向，也显示了流媒体战争下一阶段可能如何开打。

福克斯一段时间以来一直在悄然增强自身流媒体能力。2020年，它以4.4亿美元收购了Tubi，这是一项免费、广告支持的服务，用行话说就是FAST频道。它慢慢把这个平平无奇的娱乐频道发展成一个平台，在美国流媒体观看份额中占5%（见图表），与派拉蒙和NBC环球的Peacock相当。去年，它推出了Fox One，这是福克斯新闻、体育和娱乐内容的付费订阅流媒体大本营。

罗库将显著扩大福克斯的流媒体业务。罗库自家的FAST服务Roku Channel，将使福克斯在美国流媒体观看份额合计达到11%，超过迪士尼，位列YouTube和奈飞之后的第三名。有线电视仍将是福克斯的衣食所系。但流媒体如今将成为其业务组合中相当可观的一块：分析公司MoffettNathanson估计，Tubi和罗库将贡献总收入约30%；该公司认为，这笔交易是“一种确保[福克斯]未来的方式，因为未来几年流媒体将超越传统发行渠道”。

这笔交易还表明，广告正成为流媒体战争的战场。奈飞等订阅制流媒体平台起初避开广告，如今却已开始拥抱广告，因为它们要争取那些不愿每月掏20美元购买无广告套餐的会员。研究公司Antenna估计，在美国，新流媒体订户中超过一半选择广告支持套餐。广告主方面，随着传统电视收视率下滑，他们也在寻找投放广告的新去处。FAST频道正是许多广告主转向的地方。

拥有罗库将改善福克斯的广告产品。去年，罗库通过在Roku Channel以及其电视操作系统内销售广告，收入达23亿美元。除了规模，罗库还将带来一座数据宝库。去年，在美国售出的智能电视中，有800万台搭载其操作系统，超过包括三星、Alphabet和亚马逊在内的所有竞争对手。在全球，超过1亿户家庭使用罗库的流媒体技术，为其提供有关观看习惯的信息，这将帮助福克斯在所有服务中精准投放广告。在流媒体产品多年显得相对温顺之后，福克斯突然看起来颇为凶猛了。■

<h1>Fox, Roku and the next phase of the streaming wars</h1>
<h3>A $22bn deal creates a formidable new power in Hollywood</h3>
<h3>June 18th 2026</h3>
<p>One of the main winners of the streaming wars, Hollywood’s years-long content-spending free-for-all, has turned out to be a non-combatant. Fox kept its powder dry while companies such as Disney blew gazillions on building streaming services to compete with the likes of Netflix. The cable giant, which still makes most of its money from old-fashioned “linear” television, has been rewarded with a near-40% rise in its share price in the past five years, during which time Disney’s has fallen by a similar proportion.</p>
<p>On June 15th Fox took a big step into the streaming business, with the surprise announcement that it had agreed to acquire Roku, an American firm which sells streaming hardware and software and operates a streaming channel of its own. The deal, valued at around $22bn in cash and stock, signals a shift in strategy at Fox and suggests how the next phase of the streaming wars may be fought.</p>
<p>Fox has been quietly building up its streaming capabilities for a while. In 2020 it acquired Tubi, a free, ad-supported service (known in the jargon as a FAST channel), for $440m. It has slowly grown the unremarkable entertainment channel into a platform accounting for 5% of streaming viewership in America (see chart), on a par with Paramount and NBCUniversal’s Peacock. Last year it launched Fox One, a paid-subscription streaming home for all of Fox’s news, sport and entertainment.</p>
<p>Roku will expand Fox’s streaming operation significantly. Its own FAST service, the Roku Channel, will give Fox a combined share of 11% of streaming viewership in America, overtaking Disney to claim third place behind YouTube and Netflix. Cable will remain Fox’s bread and butter. But streaming will now be a serious chunk of its business mix: Tubi and Roku will contribute about 30% of total revenue, estimates MoffettNathanson, a firm of analysts, which sees the deal as “a way to ensure [Fox’s] future as streaming overtakes traditional distribution in the years ahead”.</p>
<p>The deal also demonstrates how advertising is becoming a battleground of the streaming wars. Subscription streamers such as Netflix, which initially shunned ads, have come to embrace them as they court members unwilling to shell out $20 a month for an ad-free plan. More than half of new streaming subscribers in America sign up for ad-supported plans, estimates Antenna, a research firm. Advertisers, for their part, are on the hunt for new places to sell their commercials, as viewership of traditional TV declines. FAST channels are where many are turning.</p>
<p>Having Roku will improve Fox’s ad offering. Roku made $2.3bn selling ads last year on the Roku Channel and within its TV operating system. As well as scale, Roku will bring a trove of data. Its operating system powered 8m smart TVs sold in America last year, more than any of its rivals, which include Samsung, Alphabet and Amazon. Worldwide, more than 100m households use Roku’s streaming tech, providing it with information on their viewing habits which will help Fox to target ads across all its services. After several years with a streaming offering that was comparatively tame, Fox is suddenly looking rather ferocious.<span class="span_ufinish">■</span></p>
