### 🌟 一句话核心主旨
超级流行歌手的全球巡演正从多城奔波转向少数城市长驻：哈里·斯泰尔斯、碧昂丝、Coldplay、奥利维亚·罗德里戈和爱莉安娜·格兰德等都在减少巡演城市。其核心动因是降低高企的巡演成本、提升利润，同时把旅行负担部分转移给愿意成为文化游客的歌迷。

### 🔍 核心观点与论据拆解
- 文章以哈里·斯泰尔斯为核心案例：他2014年歌词已写到频繁飞行与酒店生活；其男团和个人生涯累计在六大洲演出逾600场，上一轮巡演覆盖79个城市，但新巡演68场只安排在7个城市。
- 斯泰尔斯正在伦敦温布利体育场连演12晚，档期至7月4日结束；8月将赴纽约麦迪逊广场花园演出30场。这显示超级巨星不再主动覆盖更多地区，而是依赖歌迷跨城前来。
- 这种模式并非个例：碧昂丝的「Cowboy Carter」巡演有一半集中在3个场馆；Coldplay在部分城市密集演出；奥利维亚·罗德里戈9月开巡后也将如此；爱莉安娜·格兰德巡演仅去3个国家。
- 少城长驻让艺人更轻松也更赚钱。原文称巡演成本空前高，原因包括繁文缛节、劳动力和燃料成本上涨，以及歌迷期待复杂舞台装置，而全球运输这些设备并不便宜。
- 被选中的城市获得巨大收益。巴克莱银行称，斯泰尔斯歌迷预计仅在伦敦的门票、住宿和其他消费就达10亿英镑，即13.4亿美元；但曼彻斯特、考文垂等被排除的区域城市将失去巨星演出和粉丝消费。

原文呈现的主要张力在于利益分配不均：从艺人和主办方角度，减少城市、延长驻场可降低成本并提高利润；但对曼彻斯特、考文垂等较小区域城市而言，这意味着被全球巨星巡演边缘化。对歌迷而言，原文指出他们似乎并不介意出行，温布利观众中约25%来自伦敦以外，28%把演唱会变成短途假期，但这也意味着现场音乐体验越来越依赖旅行能力与额外消费。

### 🔮 未来趋势预判
基于原文事实，超级巨星巡演可能继续向少数大城市和大型场馆集中，长驻式、多晚连演将成为控制成本和放大利润的重要方式。与此同时，演唱会将更像现代朝圣：在音乐本身廉价且易获取的时代，现场演出因社交价值、旅行体验和偶像崇拜而变得更具吸引力。

<h1>Harry Styles and other megastars now expect you to come to them</h1>
<h3>Artists are visiting fewer cities on their global tours</h3>
<h3>June 18th 2026</h3>
<p>“STRAIGHT OFF the plane to a new hotel/Just touched down, you could never tell.” When Harry Styles sang those lyrics in 2014, he was already well accustomed to the glamour and grind of a global concert tour. Across his boyband and solo career he has performed more than 600 gigs on six continents. His <a href="https://www.economist.com/culture/2025/07/15/feather-boas-and-bald-caps-the-wacky-world-of-concert-fashion">previous tour</a> visited 79 cities.</p>
<p>But not all is as it was. The 68 concerts on his new tour will be hosted in just seven cities. He is in the midst of a 12-night run at Wembley stadium in London, which ends on July 4th. In August he will go to Madison Square Garden in New York to play 30 concerts.</p>
<p>The British heartthrob is not the only musician travelling less. Many of the biggest pop acts have started to expect their fans to come to them. <a href="https://www.economist.com/culture/2024/03/27/what-lies-behind-beyonces-country-turn">Beyoncé</a> spent half of her “Cowboy Carter” tour in just three venues. Coldplay performed extensively in certain cities; Olivia Rodrigo will do the same when she heads out on the road in September. Ariana Grande is appearing in just three countries on her tour.</p>
<p>Longer runs in fewer places mean a less gruelling experience for the artist—and a more lucrative one. Touring is more expensive than it has ever been, thanks to a combination of red tape and rising labour and <a href="https://www.economist.com/leaders/2026/05/14/oil-prices-could-soon-rise-convulsively">fuel costs</a>. (Fans expect elaborate sets: transporting them across the world is not cheap.) Staying in one place for longer is a way of keeping outlays down and profits up.</p>
<p>For the chosen cities, this means huge windfalls. Mr Styles’s fans are expected to spend £1bn ($1.34bn) on <a href="https://www.economist.com/finance-and-economics/2025/03/23/live-music-seems-recession-proof-thank-ticket-scalpers">tickets</a>, accommodation and other expenses in London alone, according to Barclays, a bank. For the smaller regional cities bumped from the schedule, such as Manchester and Coventry, it means losing access to megastars and fans’ spending power.</p>
<p>Still, fans do not seem to mind going on the road themselves. Around 25% of the fans watching Mr Styles at Wembley are travelling from outside London, and 28% are turning the occasion into a mini-break. Today’s concert-goers have become cultural tourists. In an age where music is cheap and easy to access, live shows have acquired a new social value. Concerts have become a kind of modern pilgrimage—journeys made out of devotion to an idol.  <span class="span_ufinish">■</span></p>
